About the Conference

A New Convening for the Information Ecosystem

The Penn Center on Media, Technology, and Democracy (Penn MEDIATED) is pleased to host our inaugural MEDIATED Conference — an annual convening focused on the rigorous study of the information ecosystem and its impact on democracy.

The conference endeavors to elevate cutting-edge empirical research to build a panoramic view of the media and informational landscape, illuminating the path to a healthier information ecosystem and a stronger democracy.

By centering empirical research, the MEDIATED Conference challenges assumptions widely held by advocates and policymakers, enabling interventions grounded in evidence rather than anecdote.

01
Ecosystem Scope Incorporates newspapers, radio, TV, web search, social media, podcasts, political speech, artificial intelligence, influencers, and peer-to-peer communications.
02
Cross-Sector Convening Unites academics, civil society organizations, media and journalism leaders, information technology companies, and government officials for evidence-based re-evaluation.
03
Research to Practice Bridges computational research and tools with scalable, implementable approaches to improve digital information technologies.
04
Critical Threats Addresses affective polarization, deteriorating digital public discourse, authoritarian propaganda, and the crisis of journalism.
